Tranquility Bay has real brand recognition and a domain that should be competing well in its market. What we found instead is a site that has been under sustained spam pressure since 2025, with measurable damage to its rankings and authority, and no evidence of a strategy in place to address it.

—
Spam backlink attack, unaddressed. More than 10,000 spam backlinks were pointed at the domain between 2025 and 2026, with 78% of the entire anchor profile pointing to a single automated phrase. The audit found no disavow file and no corrective action.
—
Rankings spiked and collapsed, repeatedly. Keyword counts surged multiple times throughout 2025, driven by the spam links. Google corrected each spike — pushing rankings lower than before. This cycle repeated at least four times. Both Semrush and Ahrefs data show domain rating drops aligned with each correction.
—
Technical foundation needs attention. 88 broken internal links, 92 pages in a sitewide redirect loop, and no structured data anywhere on the site — zero rich result eligibility confirmed.
—
No SEO or AI strategy was evident from the audit. No topic clusters, no active content programme, no E-E-A-T signals, no structured data. The site is not structured to perform in AI-driven search.
Section 01
Backlink Attack

Between October 2025 and March 2026, the domain received over 11,700 backlinks from 1,116 referring domains. 78% of the entire anchor profile now points to a single automated phrase — the output of a bulk link tool operating at scale. Two commercial spam networks were identified by name in the anchor data. The audit found no disavow file, no corrective link audit and no indication the issue was identified.

Section 02
Keyword Rankings

Throughout 2025, organic keyword counts surged multiple times, likely inflated by the spam activity. Each time, Google's algorithm identified and reversed the gains — pushing rankings back down and, in most cases, lower than where they started. This cycle repeated at least four times. Domain rating drops in both Semrush and Ahrefs align with each correction, indicating Google treated the link activity as a violation.

Keyword ranking positions — Semrush

The chart below shows Google correcting rankings at least four times throughout 2025. Each spike is Google temporarily registering the spam-inflated keywords before discounting them. The real ranking positions — top 3 and 4–10 — remain almost flat throughout, confirming the spikes had no genuine ranking value. Note: this is a simplified representation. The actual Semrush data shows more dramatic and volatile swings than illustrated here.

Position distribution — 8,605 ranked keywords

Of 8,605 ranked keywords, more than half — over 4,100 — sit in positions 51–100. These rankings deliver virtually no traffic. Google shows results beyond position 50 to fewer than 1% of searchers. The top 3 positions, which drive the majority of organic clicks, account for only 306 keywords. The site's organic visibility is heavily concentrated in positions where it effectively doesn't exist.

Keyword footprint vs competitors

Every direct competitor ranks two to four times more keywords than Tranquility Bay. This is a content gap, not an authority gap. A broader keyword footprint improves visibility across Google Search, Google Hotel Finder and AI-powered travel tools — all of which surface properties with greater topical depth. A smaller footprint means fewer entry points before a guest has decided where to book.

Section 06
Content &
Semantic SEO

The site's content problems are independent of the spam attack. Thin pages, duplicated structure, a homepage that underperforms on its own primary keyword, and no semantic topic coverage — these are foundational issues that would limit organic visibility regardless of the backlink profile.

Homepage grades C– on its own primary keyword

Clearscope graded the homepage C– against its own primary commercial keyword target. Clearscope measures content relevance by comparing a page's terms and topic coverage against what the top-ranking pages currently cover. A C– on the homepage — the most authoritative page on the domain — means the site is not semantically competitive on the keyword it is most trying to rank for. Competitors serving between 1,500 and 2,600 words of relevant, structured content consistently outrank it.

Content is thin, duplicated and structurally weak
—
14 pages confirmed under 200 words. Accommodation and experience pages — the commercial core of the site — are almost certainly under 500 words. Google's quality systems deprioritise thin pages in competitive queries. Competitors ranking above the site on every commercial keyword serve 3 to 5 times more content per page.
—
Duplicate and multiple H2 headings across 40+ pages. 20 pages share duplicate H2 headings across different URLs. 22 pages carry multiple H2s on a single page. These are indicators of templated or copied content — weakening each page's ability to rank independently by signalling to Google that pages lack distinct, original content.
—
No topic cluster structure. Semantic SEO requires grouped, interlinked content covering a topic from multiple angles — accommodation types, destination guides, experience pages, local area content. The site has none of this structure. It is visible only when someone already knows the hotel by name, not during the consideration and planning phase when the booking decision is actually made.
—
No local authority signals for the Florida Keys or Marathon. Local relevance signals inform both organic ranking and Google Hotel Finder placement. The site has no area guides, no local content, no geographic depth. Google cannot establish a strong local entity connection for a property that publishes nothing about where it is.
